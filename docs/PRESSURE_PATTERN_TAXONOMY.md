# Pressure Pattern Taxonomy

A pattern is a reflection cue, not a verdict.

## Core patterns

### Guilt pressure
Uses emotional debt or remorse to reduce clear choice.

### Urgency pressure
Compresses time so reflection feels impossible.

### Identity pressure
Makes belonging, loyalty, faith, family, or worth feel conditional.

### Authority pressure
Uses status or certainty to discourage honest questions.

### Care pressure
Uses concern or affection while reducing agency.

### Fear pressure
Uses future pain, regret, or threat to override reflection.

### Confusion fog
Uses contradiction, overload, or moving definitions to make clear thinking harder.

### Isolation pressure
Cuts someone away from trusted outside support or perspective.

### Shame pressure
Attacks the person instead of discussing the decision.

## Four step response

1. Notice the signal.
2. Name the possible pattern.
3. Pause safely.
4. Choose clearly.
